DIAGNOSIS (from on-server EB logs — confirmed, not theory)
================================================================================
var/log/nginx/access.log (prod):
  15:49:55  POST .../plans                    -> 413
  15:49:59  POST .../plans                    -> 413  (retry)
  15:51:10  POST .../plans                    -> 200  (small file)
  15:53:01  POST .../units/.../plans          -> 413
  15:53:05  POST .../units/.../plans          -> 413  (retry)

var/log/eb-docker/.../eb-*-stdouterr.log (app):
  15:51:10  [UPLOAD:STAGE1:RECEIVED] filename=scan-1779465061163.pdf
  → the four 413 requests have NO [REQ] / [UPLOAD] entry at all.

The 413s never reached FastAPI. nginx returns 413 only when the request
body exceeds `client_max_body_size`. proxy.conf L3 had 20M; the app
(plans_router.py L175/361/801) advertises 50MB. Anything 20-50MB was
killed at the edge → generic "שגיאה בהעלאת קובץ" toast.

Why this surfaced now: Bug 6 (doc-scanner in plans) introduced scanned
multi-page PDFs. VisionKit JPEGs are full-res, jspdf embeds them
without re-compression → a 3-5 page delivery certificate easily clears
20MB. Regular plan PDFs (5-7MB) stay under 20MB — exactly the 200/413
mix in the log. The 15:51:10 success was simply small enough.

NOT a retry/timeout issue: 413 is deterministic, and the log shows
double-retries also hitting 413. Retrying a too-large request just
produces another 413.

================================================================================
THE FIX
================================================================================
Raise nginx `client_max_body_size` to 55M — 5MB above the app's 50MB,
to absorb multipart/form-data overhead. The app stays the SINGLE real
enforcer with the proper Hebrew 422; nginx becomes a generous outer
wall that stops surprising users below 50MB. Applies uniformly to all
upload endpoints (plans, photos, contractor proofs).

App-side 50MB limit is NOT touched.

COMMIT MESSAGE (saved at .local/.commit_message)
================================================================================
fix(upload): raise nginx client_max_body_size 20M -> 55M so plan
uploads up to the app's 50MB limit are not rejected with 413

On-server EB logs showed plan uploads over 20MB returning HTTP 413
from nginx before ever reaching the app. nginx capped the request
body at 20M while the application enforces a 50MB limit, so any
upload in the 20-50MB range — including the multi-page scanned
delivery certificates added in Bug 6 — was killed at the edge with
a raw 413 and surfaced as the generic "שגיאה בהעלאת קובץ" toast.

Raise client_max_body_size to 55M, just above the app's 50MB cap,
so the application stays the single enforcer of the size limit and
returns its proper Hebrew error instead of nginx 413. Server-config
only; ships via ./deploy.sh.
