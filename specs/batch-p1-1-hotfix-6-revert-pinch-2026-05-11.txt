CONTEXT — why revert, not iterate
=================================

Three reasons to remove pinch entirely now:

1. **Pinch via re-render is fundamentally jank-prone.** Each pinch step
   triggers `setScale` → react-pdf re-renders the entire PDF canvas at
   the new scale. For a 1.6MB A1 plan, re-rasterization takes
   ~50-100ms on a mid-range iPhone. Pinch gestures fire at 60Hz
   (~16ms/frame). So we drop ~3-6 frames per gesture update — feels
   "1% at a time" instead of smooth. This isn't a config issue;
   it's architectural. The fix is the hybrid pattern (CSS transform
   during gesture, re-render once on release) — same approach Adobe
   Reader Mobile uses.

2. **Today is the wrong day to write the hybrid pattern.** 9 deploys
   across 2 days. hotfix-4 had a math bug (PATTERN A compounding).
   hotfix-5 has a fundamental perf bug. Decision quality is degrading.
   Fresh-mind work tomorrow > another hotfix tonight.

3. **Buttons work fine.** +/- buttons trigger one re-render per click
   at a known scale. Smooth, predictable, no jank. Field workers with
   gloves / dirty hands / phones in landscape often prefer tappable
   buttons over precise multi-touch gestures anyway. Field test will
   tell us if pinch is critical UX or merely nice-to-have.

What this hotfix preserves (intentionally):
  - `@use-gesture/react` stays in package.json. P1.4 will use it with
    the hybrid pattern. Removing + re-adding the dep is busy-work.
  - `touchAction: 'pan-x pan-y'` stays on the scroll container.
    One-finger pan/scroll still works natively (this is what scrollbar
    drag depends on too).
  - Math.floor fit-scale, dir="ltr", w-fit, visible scrollbars,
    multiplicative +/- buttons, thumbnail preview — all unchanged.
